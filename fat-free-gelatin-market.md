<p><p>The fat-free gelatin market consists of two primary types based on hydrolysis methods: acid hydrolysis and alkaline hydrolysis. Acid hydrolysis involves treating collagen sources with acidic solutions to break down proteins, yielding gelatin with specific properties suitable for food and pharmaceutical applications. In contrast, alkaline hydrolysis utilizes basic solutions, producing gelatin with different functional characteristics and potential uses in various industries, including cosmetics and food processing. Both methods offer unique benefits, catering to diverse market demands.</p></p>
