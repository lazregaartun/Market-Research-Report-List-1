<p><p>The stereo audio codecs market can be divided into two main types: analog and digital. Analog codecs convert sound waves into electrical signals for playback, relying on traditional technologies to reproduce audio. In contrast, digital codecs encode audio signals into binary data for processing and transmission, offering better sound quality, compression, and compatibility with modern devices. Digital codecs have gained popularity due to advancements in technology, enabling streaming and high-fidelity audio experiences, while analog remains relevant in niche markets and vintage applications.</p></p>
