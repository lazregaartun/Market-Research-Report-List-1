<p><p>The urothelial cancer drugs market comprises two main types: chemotherapy and immunotherapy. Chemotherapy involves using cytotoxic agents to target and destroy rapidly dividing cancer cells, often employed for advanced or metastatic urothelial cancer. Immunotherapy harnesses the body's immune system to recognize and attack cancer cells, utilizing agents like checkpoint inhibitors to enhance immune responses. Both treatment modalities aim to improve patient outcomes, with ongoing research driving new drug development and combination therapies in this evolving therapeutic landscape.</p></p>
